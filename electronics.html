What is an ISP PCB?

  • It allows firmware updates to be delivered to the on-chip memory of microcontrollers and related processors without requiring specialist programming circuitry on the circuit board, and simplifies design work.
  • It allows manufacturers of electronic devices to integrate programming and testing into a single production phase, and save money, rather than requiring a separate programming stage prior to assembling the system.
  • This may allow manufacturers to program the chips in their own system's production line instead of buying preprogrammed chips from a manufacturer or distributor, making it feasible to apply code or design changes in the middle of a production run.

References: Wikipedia

How to prepare your files for 2D Milling?

Step 1: Save a picture of your PCB diagram as a PNG file.
Step 2: Open up Mods.
Step 3: From Mods, under settings, click Programs >> Open Server Programs >> Mill 2D PCB PNG.
Step 4: Click here to get settings for traces.
Step 5: Or lick here to get settings for outline.
Step 6: After configuring the settings, click Calculate. This will convert the PNG file to NC file which is the G-code.
Step 7: To simulate the milling operation, open up CAMotics software. Import your NC file into the software.
Step 8: Under the 'Simulation' tab, click Run, then click Play. This will simulate the milling operation. Below is an example of the ISP traces Simulation.
