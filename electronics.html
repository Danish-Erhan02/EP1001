Electronics Design & Production

More About It

Materials

Transparency stock for laser printers (inkjet setups are likely as effective) or an A4 paper, Nitrile gloves (thicker reusable gloves are good too), Dust mask & eye protection, Good ventilation (if you can smell it – you’re breathing it!), Inexpensive photo frame (or specialized exposure frame), Red safe-light (optional – brief exposure to dim light is ok), Chemicals – positive type developer, ferric chloride, nail polish remover (or acetone), Pyrex dishes w/ lids (plastic storage containers work as well), Desk or clip lamp + ~30W CFL bulb, Drill bits, .8mm, 1mm, 1.5mm (+ others, dependent on source design), Small drillpress (or suitable dremel attachment), Misc. tools – xacto knife, metal ruler, permanent marker, vacuum, etc.

Software

The first step to designing your own PCB is to install a PCB desigining software such as EAGLE or Proteus. I have personally decided to use EAGLE to design my PCB.

Designing your PCB

The second step is to design your circuit using the tools in the software or importing schematics into the software. Here is a tutorial on how to use EAGLE software...



...After designing your PCB on the software, I can choose to print the circuit layout either on a piece of paper or on a transparency sheet depending on the material I am given.

Next, we place the transparency sheet with the printed circuit diagram on top of the photoresist board. UV light is exposed to the photoresist board. Transparent parts of the film allow light to pass through which polymerizes and hardens the resist.


The exposed core is processed through a chemical solution or developer that removes the resist from areas that were not polymerized by the light.


Then the copper is chemically removed from the core in all areas not covered by the dry-film resist. This creates the copper pattern that matches the film pattern.The core laminate surface is exposed in areas where copper was etched away.
